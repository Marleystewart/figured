Contract Components — What to Negotiate

Base Salary

Your guaranteed monthly pay. Know the league average before you sign — arriving uninformed costs you leverage. Always push upward from the opening offer.

Flight & Travel

Round-trip flights home should be in every overseas contract. If not included, budget $1,500–$3,000 out of pocket per round trip.

Housing

Some clubs provide a furnished apartment; others give a monthly allowance. Confirm which applies and get it in writing before signing.

Per Diem

Daily meal allowance during games and road travel. Ranges €25–€80 / day. Over a 10-month season this can add €3,000–€10,000 to your total package.

Guarantee

Is the contract fully guaranteed or can the club release you early? Always push for a guaranteed deal or understand precisely what the cut clause says.

Agent Fee

Standard agent commission is 5–10% of total contract value. Clarify terms before signing with any agent — an unread clause can cost you thousands.

Agent Fee Guidelines

Standard Rate

Industry standard is 5–10% of total contract value. For overseas deals requiring full placement, visa, and negotiation services, 8–10% is common.

Fee Structure

Some agents charge a flat fee per placement; others take a percentage of each monthly payment. Percentage of monthly payments is the most common structure internationally.

FIBA Registration

Agents working in FIBA-affiliated leagues should be registered. Registration protects both agent and client in international deals and is required by some leagues.

Exclusivity

Most agent agreements include an exclusivity period of 1–2 years. Draft these carefully to protect your commissions on deals you initiate.
